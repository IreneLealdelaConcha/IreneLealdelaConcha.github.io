Who I am

About me

I started studying languages because I was fascinated by how they work from the inside. I ended up designing courses for global audiences across multiple languages, and building chatbots that try to talk like people — including one about Dante Alighieri that became my Master's thesis in Digital Humanities at UCM.

The thread has always been the same: language as a design tool.

Today I work as an E-Learning Engineer at TransPerfect, where I make sure training content works — technically and linguistically — in any language.

In parallel, I keep deepening my expertise in NLP, prompt engineering and conversational design.

I know why certain structures work better for comprehension, how to design a dialogue flow that doesn't frustrate the user, and how to adapt complex content so anyone can absorb it.
